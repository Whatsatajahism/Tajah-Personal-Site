Who even are you?

Hi, User! You might have already guessed, but I'm Tajah, and my pronouns are They/Them. I have spent my entire career working with people. First, as an EFL teacher in Spain (shout out to Santiago de Compostela and Sevilla, you were great cities to live in!), then in an LGBTQ+ charity in London, where I felt my identity was getting too tied up in my day job, and most recently in a university working in racial equality, where the same thing happened again. I found that I liked education more than I liked working for an educational institution. I love people, but I don't want to make upskilling them my career. And so, I started to learn how to code, to give myself more career options, which brought me to my current Software Development Appreniceship at Mony Group.

Is this where you tell us why you'd be an asset to any team?

Yes dear reader, it is. I've been lucky enough to work in multiple sectors, with international colleagues. I know how to adapt my communication style, look at things from different perspectives, and how to hold and sustain a conversation. I am determined, from my career-switch journey to the intensity with which I approach coding problems to find solutions for our end-users. I am also adaptable and creative, which in a coding context means as much as I follow designs, I am also able to create reusable, elegant solutions to problems. This isn't to say I don't have more to learn though! I really enjoy learning from senior developers, and am excited to see what new parts of myself I uncover in the process.

Learning is what? FUNDAMENTAL!

I really do love learning, and so I want to showcase the amount of hours that have gone into the projects I've made. Hopefully, it will also show you my progress with software development. Click on the buttons above to see other things I've created. Enjoy judging me! And after you're done judging me, I implore you to ask yourselves - what have you learnt lately? What has been bringing you joy or aggravation? How are you bending your mind towards expansion and allowing the child in you to experiment? All this to say, we were all learners once, and I hope you keep that in mind while flicking through.

Interests beyond coding

Beyond coding, I'm a red belt in Taekwondo, which is a Korean martial art. I've found it to be an invaluable way to align my bodymind in a very mentally unhealthy world. I also taught myself how to use nunchuks (for tricks rather than battle), and love cooking up new recipes! Finally, I'm plucking up the courage to again call myself a writer, most recently graduating from the Harper Collins Author Academy Autumn 2023 cohort. You'll see a bit of my writing in my FAC game! I'm very much a jack of all trades, master of none, but it does mean that I usually have lots of random bits of trivia up my sleeve for any occasion! I just really like trying new things, and hope it's a quality I never lose to the capitalist machine.

Coding takes a lot of mental concentration for me, not too dissimilar to what I need to keep this nunchuk spinning without hurting myself!

Thanks for taking the time to read my peronal website. I'm more of a respond to questions type person, so I hope you enjoyed this enough to want to meet me in person!
